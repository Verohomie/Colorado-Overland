🏕️ Camp Tonight — Medano Pass Primitive Rd

🔢 21 numbered, first-come sites only (free, no reservations) starting 5.2 mi up the road. All fill on summer weekends — Jun 21 is a Sunday, so weekenders are leaving; arrive by mid-afternoon to claim one. 4WD + high clearance + low tire pressure required (deep sand & ~9 creek crossings).
🌄 Best View
Upper sites (≈ #8–#15) Higher up toward the pass — aspen pockets, openings framing the dunes and Sangres. Cooler, darker skies. 📍 37.82401, -105.45907 Free · 4WD only · best after the crossings
🛡️ Wind / Storm-Protected
Timbered lower sites (≈ #3–#7) Tucked into ponderosa that block the down-valley evening wind and give overhead cover when afternoon storms roll the ridge. 📍 ~37.785, -105.490 Free · easiest to reach · grab if windy
🔄 Easy Backup
Piñon Flats Campground Paved-access NPS campground at the dunes if all 21 sites are full or the creeks look too high. Book ahead on recreation.gov. 📍 37.7385, -105.5106 ~$20/night · reservable · any vehicle
🌅 Golden Hour
Where
From camp, walk to the nearest open rise — or drop to the main dunes and climb the first ridge. The Sangres go alpenglow-pink while the sand turns amber.
Why It Matters
The dunes are backlit and shadow-carved in the last hour — the single most photogenic light of the day, and the crowds are gone.
Worth It?
Absolutely. Bring a layer; temps drop fast once the sun clears the ridge. → Dunes lot
⚡ Quick Decisions
🌊Creeks running high→Scout on foot first; if over the hubs, take a lower site below the crossing or fall back to Piñon Flats
💨Windy evening→Pick a timbered lower site (#3–#7), not an exposed upper opening
⛈️Storm building→Off the dunes & ridges by 1 PM — lightning danger on open sand
🔢All 21 sites full→Piñon Flats CG (reservable) or San Luis SP / Alamosa for the night
⛽ Fuel Strategy
  • Top off in Walsenburg (I-25) or Alamosa — last reliable gas before the valley empties out
  • No services past the dunes entrance — fill up full
  • Air-down to ~18–20 psi at the Point of No Return for the sand
🚙 Land Cruiser Advantage
Push deeper: The full Medano Pass Primitive Rd is exactly your rig's terrain — sand, creek fords, and a 10,000-ft pass. You can continue over to Hwy 69 if you ever want the long way out.
Avoid: Don't air down too far on a loaded truck, and don't stop in the deep sand — keep momentum. Stay on the road; off-road travel is prohibited in the Preserve.
